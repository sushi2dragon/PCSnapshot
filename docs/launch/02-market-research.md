# Market Research — Comparable Products, Competitors, and Tooling

*Compiled 2026-07-16. Facts below were researched from official pricing pages, founder interviews, launch posts and forums; each carries a source link. Items marked **unverified** could not be confirmed against a primary source. Sections marked "→ Takeaway for PC Snapshot" are our own inference, not fact. No revenue figures are asserted anywhere.*

## Part A — How comparable indie desktop products won

### PureRef (reference-board tool for artists)
- Two-person studio (Idyllic Pixel, Stockholm); positioned as "a tool for artists by an artist" ([pureref.com/about_us](https://www.pureref.com/about_us.php)).
- Pay-what-you-want (including $0) for years; PureRef 2.0 (2024) added Small Business $49 flat and Business $10/mo tiers while keeping personal use free/PWYW ([CG Channel](https://www.cgchannel.com/2024/05/pureref-2-0-is-out/)).
- Growth was purely organic — word of mouth in art communities and tutorials ([Creative Bloq](https://www.creativebloq.com/how-to/pureref)).
- → Takeaway: free-for-individuals builds evangelists; monetize a *different buyer* (business use) later. But announce migration/grandfathering terms in advance — their 2.0 tier introduction caused avoidable friction.

### Raycast (Mac launcher)
- Launched 2020 for developers/power users ([Changelog interview](https://changelog.com/podcast/587)). Free-forever core; Pro $8/mo (annual) since late 2023, price held stable ([raycast.com/pricing](https://www.raycast.com/pricing)).
- Acquisition engine: an open-source extension store with 1,500+ community extensions — every contributor became a distributor ([raycast.com/developers](https://www.raycast.com/developers)). ~20k-member Slack community ([raycast.com/community](https://www.raycast.com/community)).
- → Takeaway: give the community a surface to contribute to (for PC Snapshot: public roadmap, snapshot-sharing formats, an open companion extension). Keep tiering legible — Raycast's Pro + AI add-on layering confuses buyers.

### Rectangle / Rectangle Pro (Mac window manager)
- Free MIT-licensed core (27.6k+ GitHub stars) built as successor to abandoned Spectacle; Pro is a separate **$9.99 one-time** purchase ([rectangleapp.com/pro](https://rectangleapp.com/pro/), [GitHub](https://github.com/rxhanson/rectangle)).
- Growth entirely organic: GitHub visibility + tech press ([How-To Geek](https://www.howtogeek.com/i-cant-imagine-using-my-mac-without-this-free-app/)).
- → Takeaway: the cleanest free-core/paid-Pro boundary in the set — two clearly separated offerings, no hidden paywall. Also a warning: $9.99 one-time likely caps revenue; don't underprice.

### DisplayFusion (Windows multi-monitor suite) — *closest commercial neighbor*
- Solo-founder origin (Jon Tackabury, Binary Fortress). Perpetual licenses valid for **all future versions**: Standard $34, plus Personal/Site/Enterprise tiers ([displayfusion.com/Purchase](https://www.displayfusion.com/Purchase/)). Distributed via own site + Steam ([Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/227260/DisplayFusion/)).
- Its **Window Position Profiles + Triggers** feature (save/restore layouts on dock/undock/resume) is the mature version of one slice of PC Snapshot's mechanic — buried inside a sprawling utility, documented via forum threads ([feature page](https://www.displayfusion.com/Features/WindowPositionProfiles/)).
- Acquisition: long-tail SEO from help-guide docs, each paired with a public discussion thread — support doubles as SEO and social proof ([help guide](https://www.displayfusion.com/HelpGuide/WindowPositionProfiles/)).
- → Takeaway: (1) $30-35 is a proven price point for a Windows power-user utility; (2) "all future versions forever" constrained their monetization and later price changes drew forum pushback — prefer a bounded update window; (3) their weakness is exactly PC Snapshot's strength: utility-grade UX vs. designed product.

### BetterTouchTool (Mac automation)
- Solo developer (Andreas Hegenberg) since 2013. **45-day full-featured trial, no payment details**; then $12 standard (2 years of updates) or $24 lifetime (multi-source-corroborated, not primary-fetched: [How-To Geek](https://www.howtogeek.com/why-i-think-bettertouchtool-for-mac-is-an-essential-purchase/), [MacAppHub](https://macapphub.com/apps/bettertouchtool)).
- → Takeaway: the "N years of updates, keep what you licensed" model is proven for solo devs; long no-card trials suit tools that need workday integration to prove value. Pick a durable brand/domain early (his boastr.net → folivora.ai migration cost brand equity).

### Obsidian (local-first notes) — *closest positioning neighbor*
- Launched in public beta March 2020 ([Ness Labs interview](https://nesslabs.com/obsidian-featured-tool)). Core app 100% free even commercially; revenue only from optional add-ons: Sync $4-5/mo, Publish $8-10/mo, Catalyst $25 one-time supporter tier ([obsidian.md/license](https://obsidian.md/license)).
- "Free, no account, local-first, your files are yours" is the whole value prop; 5,000+ community plugins drive acquisition ([eesel overview](https://www.eesel.ai/blog/obsidian-pricing)).
- → Takeaway: local-first is a *marketing pillar with a proven loyal audience*, not just an architecture choice. But their free-everything model needs adjacent paid services PC Snapshot doesn't have yet — copy their messaging, not their monetization.

### LocalSend (open-source AirDrop alternative)
- Solo dev, launched Dec 2022 via GitHub + Reddit (r/localsend from day one); 40k+ GitHub stars with zero marketing spend; deliberately unmonetized ([Console interview](https://console.substack.com/p/console-181), [Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/localsend)).
- → Takeaway: acquisition mechanics only — GitHub/Reddit-first launch works for "this should be built into the OS" utilities. PC Snapshot's already-source-available companion extension can play this role.

### Stardock Fences (Windows desktop organizer) — *cautionary tale*
- 30-day trial; price escalated across versions (Fences 4 ≈ $19.99, Fences 5 at $50 on Steam), and Fences 6 upgrade pricing produced a public forum dispute (advertised $19.99 vs $29.99 at checkout) plus narrowed device entitlements — users called it a rug-pull ([SteamDB](https://steamdb.info/app/607380/), [forum thread](https://forums.stardock.com/539573/fences-6-lifetime-license-pricing-confusion)).
- → Takeaway: **the single most important mistake to avoid.** Never change the deal on existing customers without explicit grandfathering, and never let advertised price ≠ checkout price.

**Finding (grounded in what's publicly indexed):** no dedicated, polished, visual, one-click product restores apps + window layout + exact browser tabs + terminal directories on Windows. The closest feature (DisplayFusion) is buried; the closest product (SmartWindows) has weak branding and trust. This is genuine white space — and it also means the *category* is unfamiliar, so marketing must teach the job-to-be-done, not just claim superiority.

## Part C — Cross-cutting patterns (evidence-backed)

1. **Local-first/no-telemetry is an acquisition pillar**, proven by Obsidian and LocalSend's loyal communities.
2. **Free core + paid layer beats a crippled core** — Rectangle, Obsidian, PureRef; the paid tier should add *new capability for a heavier user*, not remove basics.
3. **Changing the deal on existing customers is the #1 observed trust destroyer** (Fences 6; milder: PureRef 2.0). Grandfather explicitly, always.
4. **Community-as-distribution is the dominant zero-budget channel** (Raycast extensions, Rectangle/LocalSend GitHub, Obsidian plugins, DisplayFusion forum-as-SEO).
5. **Long full-featured no-card trials (30-45 days) are the category norm** for power-user desktop tools (BetterTouchTool 45d; Fences, SmartWindows, Workspaces 30d).
6. **Solo-founder tenure + public support channels substitute for marketing budgets** (PureRef, BetterTouchTool, Rectangle, LocalSend).
7. **$20-35 one-time is the repeatedly-validated price band** for a serious Windows/Mac utility (DisplayFusion $34, Fences ~$20-30, Workspaces $19.99, BTT $24 lifetime).

## Part D — Tooling stack (researched July 2026, prices cited)

Two India-specific findings shape the entire stack:
1. **Stripe is effectively unavailable to Indian entities** (invite-only since May 2024 under RBI's PA-CB framework — [globalsolo.global](https://www.globalsolo.global/blog/stripe-atlas-indian-founders-invite-only-guide-2026)), making a merchant-of-record platform the payments backbone.
2. **Azure Trusted Signing excludes individual developers outside the USA/Canada** ([Microsoft FAQ](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/artifact-signing/faq)), ruling out the cheapest code-signing path.

**Lean-stack fixed cost: ~$20-40/month (~₹1,750-3,500/mo)** — dominated by the amortized code-signing certificate; everything else is free at launch scale, with costs scaling only with revenue (Paddle's per-sale fee) or growth past generous free tiers. *(INR at an assumed ₹87/USD.)*

Other verified constraints worth noting:
- New CA/Browser Forum rules cap code-signing cert validity at ~458 days from Feb 27, 2026 ([ssl.com](https://www.ssl.com/guide/esigner-pricing-for-code-signing/)) — budget for near-annual renewal.
- SmartScreen "unrecognized app" warnings persist for 2-8 weeks after signing until download volume builds reputation ([Microsoft](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/apps/package-and-deploy/smartscreen-reputation)) — the launch plan must front-load free downloads *before* the paid launch push to burn this off.
- Lemon Squeezy is now Stripe-owned with visibly slowed development — a platform-risk argument for Paddle beyond fees.
